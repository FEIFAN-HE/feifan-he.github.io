The presence of technology has revolutionized the human race, but I did not realize their importance until I took one of my undergraduate courses.

I learned Excel in my undergraduate courses to be able to crunch numbers, build financial models, and visualize business data. It was fascinating to me as it was the first software I used to make my life easier.

Excel was great at first, but I soon realized it has its limits. It is good for working with table-based data, and it is only good for working with tables.

The experience of working with Excel has given me a taste of how software can reduce manual effort. But at the same time, I also felt constrained to work with table-based data only, and the functionalities are limited as well. This is where my learning journey began.

I wanted something that can help me automate repetitive tasks and ease my workload. I ended up teaching myself programming, and Python was my entry point.

Python was exactly what I anticipated it to be and much more. It is not only used in automating tasks with scripts, but it also opens the door to a world of knowledge in technology.

There are many Python libraries, such as Matplotlib (used for data analytics) and Pandas (used for data visualization).

I was able to get my feet wet by using Data Analytics and Data Visualization techniques to analyze CoronaVirus trends. https://github.com/FEIFAN-HE/CoronaVirusDataAnalysis

This project has taught me the background knowledge I needed to further my learning in Python. My Learning List
  1. Algorithmic trading and risk analysis (QuantConnect with Python)
  2. Web Programming(Django with Python)

I strongly believe my passion for technology will give me an advantage over my peers in my graduate studies as well as in the workforce, and help me succeed in the long run.
